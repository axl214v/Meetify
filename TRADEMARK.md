# Trademark Policy

This policy outlines the rules for using the name, logos, and branding assets associated with the **Meetify** project. Our goal is to protect the identity of the project, ensure users aren't confused by unofficial copies, and maintain the integrity of the brand.

## 1. Allowed Usage (What you CAN do)
Without explicit written permission, you are welcome to:
* **Refer to the project:** Use the name "Meetify" in plain text to describe your technology stack, refer to the official repository, or write educational content (blogs, tutorials, videos).
* **State compatibility:** State that your own third-party software is "compatible with Meetify" or "designed for Meetify", provided that it is completely true and does not imply an official endorsement.

## 2. Restricted Usage (What you CANNOT do)
You may not do the following without prior written consent from the project owners:
* **Confusingly similar naming:** Name your own software, fork, product, or service using "Meetify" as part of its name (e.g., "Meetify Pro", "Meetify Plus", or "Meetify-Node"). 
* **Domain Names & Social Handles:** Register domain names, social media handles, or community channels (Discord, Telegram, etc.) containing the word "Meetify".
* **Implying Official Status:** Use the official Meetify logo or name in a way that suggests your project, website, or business is endorsed, sponsored, or maintained by the official Meetify team.
* **App Stores:** Publish applications to the Apple App Store, Google Play, or any other marketplace using the Meetify name or official icons.

## 3. Forks and Modifications
The open-source license grants you the right to modify and redistribute the *source code* of this project. However, the trademark license does **not** transfer with the code. 
* If you fork this repository and significantly modify the code or distribute your own version, **you must remove all official Meetify branding, logos, and rename your project** to prevent user confusion.
